TOPIC: Geography and Communities — The Four Regions in Detail
SOURCES: Inuit Tapiriit Kanatami (ITK); Statistics Canada (2021 Census); Crown-Indigenous Relations and Northern Affairs Canada

INUVIALUIT SETTLEMENT REGION
- Location: Northwestern Northwest Territories, above the tree line, extending to the Beaufort Sea.
- Primary language: Inuvialuktun (three main dialects: Uummarmiutun, Siglitun, Kangiryuarmiutun).
- Land claim: Inuvialuit Final Agreement (1984) — the first comprehensive Inuit land claim in Canada.
- Main communities include Inuvik, Tuktoyaktuk, Aklavik, Paulatuk, Sachs Harbour, Ulukhaktok.

NUNAVUT
- Canada's largest territory, created on April 1, 1999, carved out of the Northwest Territories.
- Capital: Iqaluit (on Baffin Island).
- Population: approximately 25,000 Inuit across 26 communities; Inuit make up roughly 85% of the territory's population.
- Official languages of the Government of Nunavut: Inuktitut, Inuinnaqtun, English, French.
- Three administrative regions:
  • Qikiqtaaluk (Baffin) — eastern region, includes Iqaluit, Pangnirtung, Pond Inlet, Arctic Bay, Cape Dorset/Kinngait.
  • Kivalliq — central, along the western coast of Hudson Bay; includes Rankin Inlet, Baker Lake, Arviat.
  • Kitikmeot — western; includes Cambridge Bay, Kugluktuk, Gjoa Haven.
- Governance blends traditional Inuit values (Inuit Qaujimajatuqangit) with a contemporary parliamentary system.

NUNAVIK
- Location: Northern third of the province of Quebec, north of the 55th parallel.
- Primary language: Inuktitut (Nunavik dialect).
- Roughly 14 communities along the Hudson Bay, Hudson Strait, and Ungava Bay coasts — including Kuujjuaq (regional hub), Inukjuak, Salluit, Puvirnituq.
- Land claim: James Bay and Northern Quebec Agreement (1975) — the first modern land claim in Canada.

NUNATSIAVUT
- Location: Northern coast of Labrador (part of Newfoundland and Labrador).
- Primary language: Inuttut (the local Inuit language, sometimes referred to as Labrador Inuktitut).
- Land claim: Labrador Inuit Land Claims Agreement (2005), establishing Nunatsiavut as a self-governing region.
- Five communities: Nain (administrative capital), Hopedale (legislative capital), Postville, Makkovik, Rigolet.
- Approximately 4,000 people of Inuit descent live in the region.
